ANALYSES

Reference Aided DNASeq

We offer the following approach to analyse WGS data if the reference genome is readily available at public resources like NCBI, ENSEMBL, JGI or others.

  1. Alignment of the reference genome: Reads will be aligned to the reference genome to check the mappability, coverage and depth of the organism sequenced.

  2. Variation calling: For the samples, we will be performing variant calling to call SNVs, InDels and smaller complex variations like MNPs (<50bp). These will be genotyped to quantify the genetic makeup of the sample.

  3. Structural and Copy Number Variation calling: Multiple state of the art tools will be used to extract large-scale variation (>=50bp) events like Duplication, Deletion, Translocation and Inversions.

  4. Annotation and Prioritization of Variations: Variations will be annotated to assess their impact on the sequenced sample to check their deleteriousness or viability.

  5. Phylogenetic analyses: Concatenated SNPs from each sample or core genes identified by BUSCO will be used for sequence alignments and creating an SNP tree.

  6. QTL identificaion: Assuming bulked samples are present, QTLs can be identified from the called variants.

  7. GWAS: If many samples (>96) and their associated phenotypes are present, we also perform association testing to see which SNPs can play a major role.

  8. Assembly and Annotation of Unaligned reads: Unaliged reads from Step 1 are subjected to assembly to form contiguous regions and are annotated to see if they harbour new genes compared to the reference.
