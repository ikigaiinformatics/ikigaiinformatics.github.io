ANALYSES

Shotgun Metagenome Analyses

We perform Shotgun Metagenome Studies to generate the following information about the prospective communities:

  1. Data QC and Contamination screen: Low quality sequences and bases are removed. Subsequently, if the samples are obtained from a host, we remove reads related to the host sequences.

  2. Meta-Genome Assembly: Create contiguous sequence segments using the sequenced reads representing the communities. During this step, data from all the samples are pooled for the assemblies.

  3. Binning the Contigs and Curation: Contigs from the above step are segregaed into Bins based on the contig sequence properties. Curation is done by consolidating multiple binning approaches and removing contaminant sequences from the Bins.

  4. Blob Analyses: Colorful community visualization based on sequence properties (eg. GC content), nucleotide frequencies and bin abundances.

  5. Bin Reassembly: Curated bins are reassembled using strict and permissive filters via constituent reads to create a high quality Metagenome Assembled Genomes (MAGs).

  6. Taxonomic Assignments: Assign taxonomies to the curated/reassembled bins.

  7. Annotation of the Bins: Predict functional and non-functional features of the MAGs. Genes, CRISPR arrays, tRNAs, rRNAs etc are predicted.

  8. Quantification of Bins: Bins will be quantified to estimate the abundance of the MAGs.

  9. Functional Enrichment Analyses: Identify enriched functional pathways in the community, these can be KEGG, COG, CAZy, GO features etc. Study how the community is functionally interacting.

  10. Differential Abundance Analyses: Identify the significantly modulated community member(s) in the metagenome and try to assess their impacts.
