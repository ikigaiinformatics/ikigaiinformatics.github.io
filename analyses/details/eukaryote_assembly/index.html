Eukaryotic Genome Assembly

We perform Eukaryotic Genome Assemblies to generate the following information about the prospective organism.

  1. Genome Survey: Determine metrics like Genome size, heterozygosity and repetitive content of the genome.

  2. Genome Assembly: Create contiguous sequence segments using the sequenced reads representing the genome.

  3. Genome Masking: Identify low complexity, mobile elements and simple repeats to mine features.

  4. Genome Annotation: Identify and mine features coding for proteins or their regulatory elements.

  5. Phylogenetic and Gene-Evolution Analyses: Associate your species with the taxonomy and delineate what makes it different from the other species.

  6. Macro-synteny Analyses: Check how features are arranged in comparison with other species
