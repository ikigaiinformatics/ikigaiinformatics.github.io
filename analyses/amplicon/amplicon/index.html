ANALYSES

Amplicon Analyses

We offer the following approach to analyse Amplicon data if the data is from either Illumina or PacBio CCS platforms. Amplicons are genetic sequences which can be either complete ITS, 16s or 18s or partial segments forming the Hyper-Variable regions.

  1. QC and Read Trimming: Reads are trimmed to remove adapter or primer segments to retain the Amplicon sequence stretch. High quality reads are then retained post the trimming.

  2. Merging of the Reads: This is an Illumina only step, where the reads are merged to form a near-complete sequence of the targetted Hyper-Variable region. This leads to the retention of reads between 390-420bp. In case of PacBio CCS data, this is not required, but instead we retain reads greater than 1600bp as it captures the complete gene.

  3. Denoising of the Reads: Error profiles are built by pooling the samples and we tend to correct any artifactual errors still present in the reads.

  4. Deduplication of the Reads: Once the errors are removed, we use a Single Base resolution to cluster and collapse the reads generating a representative read. These are called as Amplicon Specific Variants.

  5. Chimeric Read Removal: Chimeric sequences are identified and eliminated from the Read-pool. Generally, the number of reads removed should be less than 0.5% of the entire dataset.

  6. Taxonomic Assignment: Taxonomy for Bacterial and Archaeal sequences are assigned using GTDB and for fungi using UNITE database, though this will depend on the marker gene sequenced.

  7. ASV Phylogeny: ASVs passing through the Chimeric Read Removal steps are then aligned using a Multiple-Sequence-Aligner and undergo a Phylogenetic Prediction to determine the ecology covered in the samples.

  8. Diversity Analyses: Alpha diversities like Richness, Dominance, Rarefaction, Core-Community are captured and Beta diversities like Community-disparity and Ordination are mined to tease the difference between the samples based on the ASVs quantified and taxonomic assignments.

  9. Differential Abundance: Tease out the differences between the various communities and samplings undertaken statistically. These can answer questions regarding impact of drugs on the microbiota, wash of industrial effluents etc.

  10. Functional Profiling: Based on the ASVs understand what functional traits (eg KEGG, GO, COG, CAZy etc) are enriched for the communities.

  11. Ecological Interaction: Infer co-occurence networks of the various ASVs and decipher patterns of associations between microbial taxa. Identify the key players or Hub microbes that play the most important role.
